Hi, I'm Emily

Genemod

Simplifying Laboratory Management through Intuitive Design — used by 150+ biotech and academic labs across the U.S. and Europe.

Role
UX/UI Designer II
Timeline
Dec 2023 – Dec 2025
Tools
Figma · Miro · Jira · Adobe Suite
🧬
01
Overview

As UX/UI Designer at Genemod, I led design across a scientific information management platform spanning web, mobile, and tablet. In a fast-paced startup, I wore multiple hats — from research and product design to light project management — collaborating directly with scientists.

150+
Labs on the platform
1M+
R&D activities
798K
Impressions / month
02
The Challenge

As Genemod expanded, my work spanned both proactive feature design and ongoing UX optimization — balancing new features with customer-reported tickets to deliver rapid value while maintaining visual consistency at scale.

  • Difficulty navigating between modules (Freezer, ELN, Orders, etc.)
  • Gaps in workflows — missing analytics or sample traceability
  • Outdated or inconsistent UI patterns across web and mobile
03
Research Process
1

Customer Reported Tickets

I categorized tickets by theme, prioritized by workflow impact, and converted findings into scoped UX improvements. A high-impact ticket is one that blocks a core workflow.

2

User Interviews

For major features, I coordinated interviews with scientists and lab managers. Before the Analytics Dashboard, interviews revealed that lab managers were using external spreadsheets for tracking — directly validating the need for built-in tools.

"I just want to track my team's order finances without exporting my data from Genemod into Excel." — Genemod user
3

Internal Testing & Validation

I performed QA testing on new features and completed tickets — verifying flows, identifying inconsistencies, and revalidating resolved tickets to prevent regressions.

4

Workflow Mapping

I created detailed maps across all core modules — Freezers, Consumables, Notebook, Orders, Protocols — visualizing each journey step to identify cross-module pain points.

04
Design Work
Analytics Dashboard

Gave lab managers a visual overview of sample usage, freezer capacity, and consumable stock — actionable insights at a glance, without leaving the platform.

  • High-level view of lab activity across all modules
  • Lab managers can track usage trends and freezer capacity
  • Modular data cards designed for future scalability
Barcode UI Redesign

Redesigned to support more label size options, printer resolution control, custom Barcode IDs, and flexible label content.

Move Rack & Category Feature

Eliminated the need for duplicate setup steps — enabling effortless cross-freezer reorganization for evolving lab layouts.

Freezer Item Color Coding

Introduced color-coded indicators for sample statuses, reducing time spent locating specific samples across large datasets.

05
Results & Reflection
  • Reduced post-release bugs through improved internal QA
  • Positive enterprise feedback citing improved clarity and consistency
  • Faster adoption for new modules like Analytics
  • Stronger design–engineering collaboration via structured systems

Working at Genemod taught me that great design isn't just about visual polish — it's about balance. Balancing feature innovation with ongoing improvements deepened my ability to prioritize, empathize, and design for scalability.

Around Town

An app empowering small, locally owned businesses — a community-driven resource supporting outreach and sales throughout the Covid-19 pandemic.

Role
Project Manager, Designer
Team
E. Blakeman · M. Luu · S. Huang · K. Dao
Type
Mobile App Design
🏘️
01
Overview

Due to Covid-19, many small businesses struggled or shut down. As restrictions tightened and capacity limits reduced foot traffic, these businesses — and the families that depend on them — faced serious hardship.

"To support one's community is to support the people that helped shape everything around you." — Emily Blakeman

Goal: A platform that encourages community members to support local businesses through incentives, bios, reviews, and location services.

02
Core Features
Search

Category filters (food, clothing, online stores). Friend search adds social discovery to local business finding.

Promotions

Businesses list their own deals — from percentage discounts to punch card rewards. Users see what's nearby at a glance.

Social

Follow friends and see their reviews in a news feed. Social proof motivates users to visit businesses their community loves.

03
Design Process

We moved through structured phases — starting with low-fidelity wireframes to explore structure and flows, then into a high-fidelity design system with a cohesive visual language built around trust and accessibility.

Style Guide
#4A75E4 — Primary Blue #ECDA9B — Warm Yellow #A5C9FF — Sky Blue

The high-fidelity design covered onboarding, account creation, search, business profiles, reviews, social feeds, promotions, and personalized settings.

04
Key User Flows
  • Get onboarded as a consumer or business user
  • Search and discover local businesses by category
  • View business profiles with reviews and hours
  • Create and post reviews to earn points
  • Browse and activate promotions and deals
  • Socialize with friends, view their recommendations
  • Personalize settings for content and visibility

Alumnify

A web application facilitating networking, mentorship, and career development between alumni and students through mutual interests and community groups.

Role
UX/UI Designer
Timeline
Jan – Mar 2023
Team
E. Blakeman · E. Eckey · E. Yu · K. Dao
🎓
01
Overview

Alumnify facilitates networking and engagement between users through mutual interests and groups — enabling mentorship, career development, and community connection for alumni, students, and university partners.

Alumni

Lost connectedness post-graduation — want to stay involved and mentor current students.

Students

Looking for mentorship and employment opportunities as they plan post-university life.

University Leadership

Advisors, partners, and employers who need to understand alumni career paths and identify partnerships.

02
Research

We defined the problem by identifying that alumni lack post-graduation connection pathways, students lack mentorship structures, and university leadership lacks visibility into alumni outcomes.

Synthesis & Ideation

Each team member wrote key terms on sticky notes, organized into clusters by category. We voted on the most important factors — ensuring design decisions were grounded in research, not assumption.

03
Design

We designed a user-friendly, simplistic, and informative platform covering the full experience — from onboarding through daily social interaction:

  • Login and role-based onboarding for alumni, students, and staff
  • User profiles with career history and interests
  • Interest group creation, posting, and event management
  • Private messaging and notification system
  • Search with filters for users and groups
  • Customizable settings for visibility, content, and multimedia
  • University staff views for database and marketing management
04
Reflection
1

Center Design Around User Research

Understanding users' needs led to features genuinely tailored to their desires — not what we assumed they wanted.

2

Accessibility is Always a Priority

Extra settings for visibility, content filters, and multimedia opened the platform to a diverse range of abilities and enhanced the experience for everyone.

3

Team Timelines Are Tremendously Helpful

Creating per-phase deadlines kept everyone accountable across different schedules and gave us buffer time for quality improvements.
